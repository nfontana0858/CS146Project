What are Groups?

Groups allow you to collaborate and coordinate schedules with family, friends, classmates, or coworkers. Create shared calendars, assign tasks, and keep everyone on the same page with important events and deadlines.

Types of Groups

Family Groups

Coordinate family events, appointments, chores, and activities. Keep track of everyone's schedules in one shared space.

Study Groups

Plan study sessions, track assignment deadlines, and coordinate project work with classmates and teammates.

Work Teams

Manage project timelines, meeting schedules, and team deadlines. Stay aligned on important work milestones.

Social Groups

Plan events, outings, and gatherings with friends. Coordinate schedules to find the best times for group activities.

Group Features

  • Shared Calendars: View and edit events that affect the entire group
  • Task Assignment: Assign specific tasks and responsibilities to group members
  • Event Planning: Coordinate group events and find optimal meeting times
  • Progress Tracking: Monitor group goals and project milestones together
  • Notifications: Get alerts about group events, deadlines, and updates
  • Color Coding: Distinguish between different groups with custom colors

Getting Started with Groups

  1. Create a Group: Set up a new group and give it a name and description
  2. Invite Members: Add people to your group by email or username
  3. Set Permissions: Choose who can edit events, assign tasks, and manage the group
  4. Start Planning: Begin adding shared events, deadlines, and goals
  5. Stay Connected: Use the group calendar to keep everyone informed and organized

Example Uses

Planning a Group Project

Create a study group for your CS-146 class. Add project milestones, meeting times, and individual task deadlines. Track progress and ensure everyone stays on schedule for the final presentation.

Family Coordination

Set up a family group to coordinate schedules. Add everyone's work hours, school events, appointments, and family activities. Never miss another important event or double-book yourself.

Weekend Trip Planning

Create a social group for planning a weekend getaway. Coordinate travel times, book accommodations, plan activities, and ensure everyone knows the itinerary.
